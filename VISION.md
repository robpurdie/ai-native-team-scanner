# Vision: Why Detect AI-Native Teams?

## The Problem

Organizations investing heavily in AI tooling often see uneven adoption and value realization. Some teams transform their work; others apply new tools to old methods. The difference isn't tool access—it's whether teams have developed the **adaptive mindset, collaborative behaviors, and engineering discipline that AI demands**.

Without a way to identify which teams are genuinely AI-native, organizations struggle to:

- **Find exemplars** for peer learning and knowledge sharing
- **Understand adoption patterns** across hundreds of teams
- **Allocate support resources** effectively
- **Measure capability development** over time
- **Avoid cargo cult transformation** (superficial adoption without underlying capability)

## The Insight

AI-native teams aren't just using AI tools—they're being **pulled toward better engineering practices** by those tools. The connection runs both ways:

- AI coding assistants work better with clean, well-tested code
- Teams with strong test coverage can safely iterate faster with AI
- Good documentation makes AI suggestions more accurate
- Clear commit practices make AI-generated code easier to review

**AI adoption and engineering discipline are inseparable.** Teams getting the most value from AI are simultaneously improving both.

## The Opportunity

GitHub repositories contain **observable signals** of team-level AI-native working:

- Tool configuration files (`.cursorrules`, Copilot instructions)
- AI-generated content markers in commits and documentation
- Test file presence and growth patterns
- Conventional commit adoption
- Code review patterns
- Documentation quality

These signals can be detected **programmatically** across hundreds of teams, enabling organizations to:

1. **Baseline current state** - Understand AI adoption landscape
2. **Identify exemplars** - Find teams worth learning from
3. **Target support** - Focus coaching where it will have most impact
4. **Track progress** - Measure capability development over time
5. **Share patterns** - Extract learnings from high-performing teams

## The Approach

A **maturity model** that assesses teams on two inseparable dimensions:

1. **AI Adoption** - How teams are integrating AI into workflows
2. **Engineering Practices** - Foundation that makes AI effective

Teams are scored on both dimensions, with the **lower score** determining overall maturity level. This encoding principle reflects that both dimensions must develop together.

Three levels:
- **Level 0: Not Yet** - Below threshold on one or both dimensions
- **Level 1: Integrating** - Meeting thresholds, building capability
- **Level 2: AI-Native** - Sustained excellence across both dimensions

## The Context

This methodology was developed at Cisco IT, a 10,000-person organization with 500+ Agile teams and a small coaching team operating through a "coach the coaches" model. The challenge: how do ~10 coaches help hundreds of teams develop AI-native capability when direct observation doesn't scale?

The answer: **programmatic detection** that surfaces teams worth deeper engagement, combined with a **leadership cascade** approach where domain leaders develop their teams.

## Success Looks Like

**Short term:**
- Baseline understanding of AI adoption across IT
- 5-10 exemplar teams identified for peer learning
- Coaching resources focused on highest-leverage opportunities

**Medium term:**
- Quarterly tracking of capability development
- Patterns and practices extracted from high-performing teams
- Leadership conversations grounded in data, not anecdotes

**Long term:**
- Measurable increase in AI-native teams (Level 2)
- Sustained engineering practice improvements
- Culture shift from "doing Agile" to "being Agile" with AI

## What This Is NOT

This is not:
- Individual developer tracking or surveillance
- A performance management tool
- A comprehensive measure of all AI usage (many teams work outside GitHub)
- A substitute for outcome measurement (velocity, quality, satisfaction)
- A one-time audit (requires ongoing measurement and calibration)

## The Bigger Picture

AI-native working isn't about tools—it's about **adaptive capability**. Teams that can integrate new capabilities quickly, learn collectively, and improve their practices continuously will thrive regardless of which specific AI tools emerge.

This methodology helps organizations **recognize and develop** that capability at scale.
